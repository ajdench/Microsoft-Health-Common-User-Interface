# Research Scope Status

<blockquote>
<p><strong>Plain English context</strong></p>
<p>This page answers the handoff question: have we pushed the historical/discovery phase far enough that moving on to modern design-system authoring is now the better use of time?</p>
</blockquote>

## What is now well covered
- The toolkit source mirror is localized.
- The main bundled design-guidance corpus is localized and normalized.
- The standards/deprecation/archive branch is localized and documented.
- The programme-site distribution/governance surface is localized as far as the surviving archive seems to allow.
- The adoption story now includes named evidence for EMIS, System C, Aintree, iLink, CareWorks, Civica, and Valencia/ABUCASIS.
- The showcase branch is partially reconstructable from localized toolkit code and a small number of public traces.

## What remains unresolved
- Full CodePlex release attachments and readmes are still not directly retrievable.
- Exact deployment depth across every named supplier remains unprovable.
- The live Silverlight showcase experiences and media files are not preserved here.
- Some historical web branches almost certainly vanished before preservation.

## Why the remaining gaps are probably low leverage
- The missing pieces are now mostly edge artefacts, not missing conceptual foundations.
- They are unlikely to materially change the modern interpretation of:
  - patient context
  - medication display and prescribing
  - alerts and decision support
  - dense clinical data handling
  - design-system governance for clinician-facing software
- The current corpus is already strong enough to support practical React/design-system translation work.

## Practical recommendation
- Treat the historical discovery phase as substantially exhausted.
- Keep future research opportunistic rather than exploratory by default.
- Move primary effort toward modern design-system authoring and implementation contracts.
