# User-Centred Design

## Retrieved facts
- The toolkit README says the design guidance was produced through a rigorous user-centred design process.
- The sample website’s patient-safety page lists clinician and provider involvement in personas, wireframe review, prototype assessment, and assessment of implemented guidance.
- The same page states that healthcare providers, patient-safety experts, and software providers all took part in structured research activities.

## Synthesis
- Health CUI was historically positioned as a research-backed design programme, not merely a standards edict.
- The corpus repeatedly connects guidance quality to validation with real clinical work, not abstract UI taste.

## Modern interpretation
- The strongest modern lesson is methodological: clinician-facing design systems need research and governance around real care tasks, not just generic component consistency.
- Inference: a modern equivalent would combine design-system stewardship with clinical-safety review, usability research, and domain-specific acceptance criteria.

## What this means for later work
- Pattern proposals in this repo should be tested against workflow realism.
- Modern mappings should distinguish between reusable safety heuristics and historically specific implementation decisions.
