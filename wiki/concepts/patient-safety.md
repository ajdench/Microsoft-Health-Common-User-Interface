# Patient Safety

## Retrieved facts
- The sample website states that patient safety was paramount to the design and development of the guidance and toolkit controls.
- The patient-safety material describes research with clinicians, patient-safety experts, and software providers, plus iterative risk assessment across the lifecycle.
- Multiple guidance documents tie formatting and layout choices directly to reducing misidentification, medication errors, and misunderstanding of clinical status.

## Concrete safety themes in the corpus
- Consistent patient identification and persistent patient context.
- Clear separation and labeling of medication attributes such as dose, route, frequency, and strength.
- Alerting and decision-support behaviours that prioritize clarity and controlled interruption.
- Visible handling of ambiguity, absence, or unavailable data, especially for allergies and identifiers.

## Synthesis
- Health CUI treats UI consistency as a clinical-safety mechanism, not only a usability or branding concern.
- The corpus assumes that the same clinician may move across multiple applications and that inconsistency itself creates risk.

## Modern interpretation
- A modern clinician-facing system should preserve this safety stance by treating component behaviour, copy, defaults, and workflow transitions as safety-critical.
- This is especially relevant for patient-switching, medication review, warning presentation, and filtering/sorting states that can silently change what a clinician is seeing.

## Uncertainty
- The repo currently has strong evidence for patient-safety framing, but not yet a fully reconstructed set of the named patient-safety principles image/diagram referenced by the sample website.
