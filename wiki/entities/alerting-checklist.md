# Alerting Checklist

## Use this when
Designing or reviewing alert panels, warning summaries, and decision-support status in a clinical UI.

## Checklist
- Communicate whether decision support is active, inactive, or incomplete in the current context.
- Group alerts by priority rather than presenting one undifferentiated stream.
- Reserve interruptive behavior for genuinely high-risk or time-sensitive situations.
- Default high-priority alerts to greater visibility than lower-priority alerts.
- Pair short summaries with accessible explanation and evidence detail.
- Make it clear when an alert reflects clinical content versus system capability/status.
- Tell users when automated coverage is partial or unavailable.
- Avoid making dismissal look equivalent to clinical resolution unless the workflow really supports that meaning.
- Ensure keyboard access and screen-reader clarity for expanded/collapsed alert details.
