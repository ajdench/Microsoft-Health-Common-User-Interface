# Patient Banner Checklist

## Use this when
Designing or reviewing a persistent patient-context header for a clinician-facing application.

## Checklist
- Show a stable patient-context area whenever a single patient record is open.
- Include a minimum identity cluster that covers patient name, date of birth, gender/current gender presentation, and patient identifier.
- Distinguish primary identity information from supplementary context so core identifiers do not get pushed out of view.
- Make deceased-patient state unmistakable without relying on color alone.
- Avoid truncating patient name or identifier where full display is feasible.
- Keep allergy or caution context near the banner only if it does not dilute identity prominence.
- Make record-switching or context changes visibly confirm the new patient.
- Ensure keyboard and screen-reader access to any expandable context details.
- Treat responsiveness as hierarchy preservation, not literal recreation of the historical desktop layout.
