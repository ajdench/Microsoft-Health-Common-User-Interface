# Standards To Guidance Crosswalk

<blockquote>
<p><strong>Plain English context</strong></p>
<p>This page shows where formal ISB standards ended and broader Health CUI guidance continued. That matters because modern teams should not assume the whole corpus had the same governance weight.

| Formal standard | Closest broader guidance | What the formal layer covers | What the broader layer adds |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `ISB 1505 Patient Banner` | Patient Banner guidance, crib sheets, micro banner guidance | Minimum content, grouping, placement, and labeling expectations | Deceased-patient handling, supplementary cues, banner variants, richer design rationale |
| `ISB 1506 Patient Name Input and Display` | Patient Name guidance and QIG | Name-entry/display standardization | Detailed treatment of name components and display choices in context |
| `ISB 1504 NHS Number Input and Display` | Patient identification number guidance and crib sheet | Identifier formatting/display | Broader patient-identification pattern alongside name, DOB, and banner |
| `ISB 1500 Address Input and Display` | Address guidance and QIG | Standardized address formatting/input | UK/non-UK variants and implementation detail |
| `ISB 1501/1502/1503` | Date/time guidance and crib sheets | Date/time display and entry rules | Richer implementation detail and examples |
| `ISB 1507 Sex and Current Gender Input and Display` | Demographic guidance and crib sheet | Standardization of sex/current gender fields | Contextual display and entry detail |
| `ISB 1508 Telephone Number Input and Display` | Telephone number guidance and QIG | Telephone formatting/input | Implementation detail and example patterns |</p>
</blockquote>

## What sits outside the formal standards family
- Medication rendering and prescribing
- Alerting and decision support
- Clinical table state visibility
- Search and patient finding
- Navigation and clinical noting

## Practical implication
- If we are doing historical provenance work, the standards family tells us what was formally governed.
- If we are doing modern clinician-facing design work, the broader guidance corpus is where much of the highest-value workflow detail lives.
