## Short summary
This document explores how clinical applications should communicate decision-support capability, present information windows, surface recommendations, and handle unprompted system-generated alerts.

## Key extractable insights
- The document distinguishes between informing, recommending, and alerting rather than treating decision support as one homogeneous interaction.
- It proposes explicit communication of whether decision support is active, grouped alert containers, and different default states for high-priority versus lower-priority alerts.
- It is already concerned with interruption cost, status visibility, and the need to avoid over-trusting automation.

## Should this influence modern reinterpretation?
Yes, but as exploratory input. It should inspire modern alerting and status patterns while remaining clearly marked as an inference source rather than settled historical standard.
