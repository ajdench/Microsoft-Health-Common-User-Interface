## Short summary
This 2019 blog post argues that the original CUI had good intentions and strong user-centred roots, but had become outdated because devices, design methods, and health-service contexts had changed.

## Key extractable insights
- Strongly reinforces that usability is treated as a clinical safety issue.
- Suggests that the enduring value lies in certain data elements and safety concerns rather than the exact historical presentation model.
- Helps explain why the repo should focus on careful reinterpretation instead of direct reuse.

## Should this influence modern reinterpretation?
Yes, especially when deciding what to preserve conceptually and what to leave behind.
