## Short summary
This guidance addresses how to filter, sort, and group clinical data safely and consistently, with special emphasis on notifying users when they are viewing a subset rather than the full data set.

## Key extractable insights
- The document frames filtering, sorting, and grouping as cognitive aids for dense clinical data rather than generic data-grid conveniences.
- It repeatedly emphasizes that clinicians must be told when the display is a subset of available data.
- It provides a useful bridge from historical Health CUI thinking into modern table, list, and result-state design.

## Should this influence modern reinterpretation?
Yes. It is especially relevant for modern clinician-facing grids, results lists, medication views, and other information-dense pages.
