## Trustworthiness / caveats
- These are later NHS programme documents, not original CUI artefacts.
- They are highly useful because they show that CUI requirements persisted into downstream requirements writing by 2015.

## Short summary
These documents show that, several years after publication of the CUI standards, later NHS requirements still said the CUI demographic standards `MUST` be followed and that broader CUI guidance `SHOULD` be followed.

## Key extractable insights
- The EPaCCS document says CUI standards `1500 to 1507` must be followed for demographic input and display.
- It also says CUI guidance should be followed more broadly, including navigation and clinical noting.
- The same requirements block sits alongside WCAG accessibility requirements, showing an overlap period where CUI and newer web-accessibility requirements were both being invoked.

## Should this influence modern reinterpretation?
Yes. It is important evidence for the programme’s afterlife and helps explain why NHS Digital later needed a formal deprecation process.
