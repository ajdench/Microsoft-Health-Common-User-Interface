## Short summary
The compliance pack explains how suppliers and NHS organizations were expected to approach CUI CAPS compliance. The checklist translates the design guidance into auditable rows with IDs, statuses, and yes/no/NA response structure across domains such as address, time display, patient banner, patient name, and telephone number.

## Key extractable insights
- The compliance material shows that Health CUI was intended to be assessed operationally, not only read as guidance.
- The checklist is especially valuable because it turns narrative guidance into a structured rule inventory.
- The pack explicitly ties Health CUI work to patient safety, clinician effectiveness, and a compliance process involving suppliers and trusts.

## Should this influence modern reinterpretation?
Yes. These artefacts are strong evidence that modern clinician-facing design systems should include review criteria and conformance mechanisms, not just component examples.
