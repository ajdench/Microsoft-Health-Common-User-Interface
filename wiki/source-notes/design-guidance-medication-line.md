## Short summary
This guidance sets detailed formatting rules for representing a single medication safely and consistently in a UI, including labels, separators, wrapping, truncation, line spacing, dose display, and attribute ordering.

## Key extractable insights
- It is explicitly motivated by medication incidents involving wrong medicine, wrong dose, wrong strength/frequency, and omitted medicine.
- It mandates that dose be labeled with the word `DOSE`, that medication information wrap rather than truncate, and that spacing and separators preserve readability.
- It treats consistent formatting as a safety mechanism for dense review workflows, not just a typographic preference.

Yes. It is one of the strongest documents in the corpus for deriving concrete component and renderer rules.
