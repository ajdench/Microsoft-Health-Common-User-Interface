## Short summary
These sources materially strengthen the supplier-uptake story. System C said Medway Sigma became the first trust-wide UK product available to standardize on the new CUI, and Digital Health reported a trust-wide Aintree deployment with more than 1,000 staff already switched over.

## Key extractable insights
- System C said Medway Sigma became the first trust-wide UK product available to standardize on the new CUI developed jointly by Microsoft and the NHS.
- The Aintree report says more than 1,000 staff, including 700 clinicians, had switched to the system, with up to 3,000 expected by early 2010.
- The Aintree report says the Sigma suite incorporated significant elements of the Microsoft-developed CUI.
- This is stronger than generic supplier-interest evidence because it ties CUI usage to named product lines and at least one named trust deployment.

## Should this influence modern reinterpretation?
Yes. It helps show that Health CUI ideas were not only published but also embodied in real clinician-facing product deployments.
