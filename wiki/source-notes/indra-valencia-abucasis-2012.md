## Short summary
This source turns the earlier Valencia lead into a real localized source. It says Indra adapted Microsoft Health Common User Interface principles to the Valencia context for the ABUCASIS ambulatory-record programme.

## Key extractable insights
- The article says Microsoft Health Common User Interface principles were adapted to the Valencia scenario.
- It frames that adaptation around patient-safety conditions, medical-attention criteria, and information presentation that avoids errors.
- It says ABUCASIS supported the electronic health record for 98% of the Valencian population and served more than 15,000 daily users.
- It therefore provides evidence of an international or at least non-NHS England adaptation path for Health CUI thinking.

## Should this influence modern reinterpretation?
Yes, cautiously. It suggests the programme's safety and ergonomics ideas traveled beyond the original English NHS context, but the source is still a vendor account and should not be treated as neutral proof of outcomes.
