## Short summary
This document covers the end-to-end prescribing interaction around quick lists, progressive search, result prioritization, cascading lists, required attributes, prescription forms, and preview/authorise stages.

## Key extractable insights
- It is oriented around minimizing mis-selection and ambiguity during prescribing rather than maximizing generic search flexibility.
- It recommends progressive search, explicit prioritization sections, spelling-match and synonym handling, and cascading selection where the choice structure itself reduces risk.
- It treats preview and authorisation as separate safety steps rather than incidental buttons at the end of a form.

## Should this influence modern reinterpretation?
Yes, with reinterpretation. The concepts are highly relevant, but the exact mechanics should be mapped carefully to modern web interaction patterns and current e-prescribing workflows.
