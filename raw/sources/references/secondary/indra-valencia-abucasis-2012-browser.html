Indra puts the Valencian Community at the forefront of digital health

February 13, 2012
Share on XShare on LinkedIn

The outpatient management system developed by Indra for the Valencian Health Agency, ABUCASIS, will incorporate new developments that will facilitate its use and integration with national and European health systems, improving the quality of care and cost savings.

ABUCASIS supports the Electronic Health Record for over 98% of the Valencian population, providing coverage to all primary care centers in the region and hospital outpatient clinics, benefiting more than 15,000 users every day

Indra, the leading IT multinational in Spain and one of the main players in Europe, will modernize the ABUCASIS outpatient management system, developed by the company for the Valencian Community, to adapt this tool to the current needs of medical professionals and facilitate their access to available patient information, not only in the Valencian Health Agency's (AVS) own systems, but also in those of other national and European regions. The project has a three-year implementation period.

Indra will evolve the system through key technological components that will enable a significant leap forward in user-friendliness (professionals will have access to more information more quickly and easily) and in the consumption of technological resources (the improvements for the user will not require additional investment in infrastructure and systems). The implementation involves more than 50,000 hours of work dedicated exclusively to the system's evolution, complemented by an equal number of hours for maintaining the current application during the process, as well as for providing support and training to professionals.

As a first step in this technological evolution, a consulting phase was conducted to review all the care processes involved in outpatient care. The results of the consulting have been incorporated into a prototype and a proposed evolution plan to be implemented over the next two years. The first production rollout of the \"evolved\" components will reach users during the first half of 2012.

Indra, in accordance with the Valencian Health Agency (AVS), is dedicating significant efforts to ensuring the tool's adaptation to healthcare processes and facilitating its viability. In this regard, the principles of the Microsoft Health Common User Interface study have been adapted to the Valencian context. This study aims to analyze the security conditions of medical organizations worldwide based on healthcare criteria, presenting health information in a way that prevents errors. Furthermore, randomly selected end users of the application have participated in working sessions to provide their feedback on the suitability of the health information presentation mechanisms.

The project aims to add value to healthcare management by providing medical professionals with all the information they need quickly and efficiently, while also enhancing integration and interoperability with other systems. It's important to remember that ABUCASIS can provide healthcare professionals with patient information from hospital procedures, diagnostic tests ordered in any healthcare setting, and even biometric data collected in patients' homes.

Indra's evolution also includes a new professional desktop that will allow physicians to organize their daily activities simply and visually, providing them with a notification inbox to facilitate patient follow-up. The project also includes a significant improvement in information retrieval within medical records, enabling the application of more innovative procedures.

\tFurthermore, the new system relies on modern design strategies geared towards the agile handling of large information structures such as those found in healthcare environments.

ABUCASIS was developed by Indra to unify outpatient medical records across the Valencian Community and currently supports the Electronic Health Record for 98% of the Valencian population. It covers all primary care centers in the region, as well as hospital outpatient clinics, serving more than 15,000 users daily. Today, it manages 1,051 centers across 24 health departments, 5.1 million patients (98% of the Valencian population), and nearly five million medical records.

Health collaboration

Indra has been collaborating with the Valencian Community in the area of ​​healthcare for years, and this partnership has resulted not only in the ABUCASIS system, but also in other developments that have positioned the Valencian region at the forefront of technological innovation in healthcare . One example is the GAIA Pharmaceutical Services Manager, which incorporates all electronic prescribing services; another is the Electronic Health Record system of the Valencian Health Agency, which has allowed healthcare centers of the Generalitat (Valencian Government) to access the health records of all citizens wherever they receive care and, at the same time, make the information they need available to them through various channels, such as mobile phones or the Citizen Portal of the Ministry of Health. Other notable examples include the Nominal Vaccine Registry and the food safety system of the Directorate General of Public Health.

Thanks to all of this, the Valencian Community is a pioneer in the Digital Health Record interoperability project within the National Health System (HCDSNS), which will allow healthcare professionals and patients to access clinical information from anywhere in Spain. In fact, Valencia is the leading Spanish region in terms of the volume and types of documents that can be consulted in other regions, with nearly 3.5 million clinical reports already integrated into the central node of the Ministry of Health, Social Policy and Equality, which promotes this initiative within the framework of the eHealth Action Plan in Europe.

Furthermore, the Valencian Community will also be the first region in Spain to integrate the management of all its laboratory services electronically thanks to the project that Indra is developing for the Valencian Health Agency in collaboration with the Alicante-based company Cointec.

Indra is the leading multinational IT company in Spain and one of the main players in Europe and Latin America. It is the second largest European company in its sector in terms of R&D investment, with over €500 million invested in the last three years. Sales in 2010 reached €2.557 billion, and its international business now accounts for 44% of its total operations. Indra employs more than 35,000 professionals and serves clients in over 110 countries.
