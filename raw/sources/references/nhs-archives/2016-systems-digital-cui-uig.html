User interface guidance

Guidance documents contain sets of criteria which should be adhered to when designing web based or standalone applications for healthcare professionals.

The development lifecycle for the design guides includes an integrated clinical safety management process. Until the design guide has received full Clinical Authority to Release from the Clinical Safety Group following a review of an approved Clinical Safety Case, outstanding clinical risks may still exist.

Healthcare system suppliers who implement design guidance should undertake further clinical safety risk assessments during implementation within specific systems and context of use.

Some of our guidance has been issued as DSCNs by the Information Standards Board. The specifications for these standards can be downloaded from the ISB wesbite and are also available below.

Access the Guidance

A number of guidance documents have been created to accompany the standard specifications. In particular for each standard there is a Design Guide and a Quick implementation Guide. These are all available on the ISB website.

Patient identification and information Entry and Display

The patient banner and related guidance on information, entry and display are key building blocks for clinical user interfaces. The standards below detail safety and usability guidance for consistent and safe presentation in the NHS.

These standards are mandated by the NHS Information Standards Board for health and social care for compliance in 2015. Further detail for each standard can be accessed in the CUI section on the ISB website.

Medications management
